## Executive Summary

This document maps the complete user journeys for Omnify AI Marketing Brain's **validated target customers**: mid-market DTC/hybrid brands in Beauty, Supplements, and Wellness sectors with $50M-$100M annual revenue.

**Three Decision Makers:**
1. **Sarah Martinez (CMO)** - Primary Economic Buyer (Decision Authority: 10/10)
2. **Jason Li (VP Growth)** - Secondary Buyer/Champion (Decision Authority: 9/10)
3. **Emily Chen (Director Performance Marketing)** - Influencer (Decision Authority: 7/10)

**Key Journey Insight**: All three personas experience the same core pain (attribution chaos, no predictions, reactive optimization) but require different microcopy and emphasis in the product experience.

## Validated Persona Profiles

### Persona 1: Sarah Martinez - CMO (Primary Economic Buyer)

**Profile**:
- **Title**: Chief Marketing Officer
- **Company**: $285M Beauty Subscription brand (Skincare/Cosmetics DTC + Hybrid)
- **Team**: 50 marketing FTE (20 in core team, 30 in operations/support)
- **Monthly Ad Spend**: $1.2M across Meta (45%), Google (30%), TikTok (20%), other (5%)
- **Current Tools**: HubSpot (CRM), Shopify (Commerce), TripleWhale (Attribution), Meta/Google/TikTok Ads Managers, Klaviyo (Email)
- **Reporting Structure**: Reports to CEO and Board, presents monthly marketing performance

**Critical Pain Points** (Ranked by Urgency):
1. **Attribution Failure (10/10 severity)**: Opens 6 conflicting dashboards every morning. Meta says ROAS 3.2, Google says 1.1, TripleWhale says something else. CEO asks "Why did CAC jump?" and there's no clean answer.
2. **Board Reporting Hell (9/10 severity)**: Spends 4+ hours weekly preparing board reports. Data doesn't match between platforms. Constantly explaining discrepancies instead of strategy.
3. **Reactive Optimization (8/10 severity)**: Finds out creative fatigued AFTER performance dropped. Lost $50K+ last quarter because no advance warning.
4. **No Strategic Visibility (8/10 severity)**: Can't answer "Should we spend more or less?" with confidence. Budget decisions feel like guesswork.

**Goals**:
- Report clearly to CEO and Board with one source of truth
- Make confident budget allocation decisions backed by data
- Predict issues 7-14 days before they impact revenue
- Reduce wasted ad spend by 15-20% ($180K-$240K annually)
- Get promoted to SVP Marketing

**Job-to-be-Done**: "Here's the truth - not what Meta thinks. Here's what will break if you don't act. Here's where to move budget tomorrow. One screen, CEO-ready."

**Buying Behavior**:
- Decision Authority: **10/10** (Controls martech budget, approves all purchases)
- Procurement Process: Can approve $499-$1,499/month SaaS without IT/Legal (under $2K/month threshold)
- Decision Timeline: 2-4 weeks (fast for mid-market)
- Key Objection: "How is this different from TripleWhale or Northbeam?"

### Persona 2: Jason Li - VP Growth (Secondary Buyer/Champion)

**Profile**:
- **Title**: VP Growth
- **Company**: $220M Cosmetics DTC/Hybrid brand
- **Team**: 35 marketing FTE (growth, creative, analytics)
- **Monthly Ad Spend**: $950K across Meta, TikTok, Google
- **Current Tools**: Salesforce (CRM), Shopify, Triple Whale, Northbeam (considering), Meta/Google/TikTok
- **Reporting Structure**: Reports to CMO, owns CAC/LTV/ROAS metrics

**Critical Pain Points**:
1. **Dashboard Fatigue (10/10 severity)**: Refreshes Meta Ads Manager every 15 minutes. Three monitors. Can't sleep when performance fluctuates without reason.
2. **Creative Fatigue Blindness (9/10 severity)**: Creatives die suddenly. Team scrambles to replace. No advance warning = wasted spend.
3. **LTV Drift Anxiety (8/10 severity)**: Cohort LTV dropping but can't predict which new customers will churn. Acquisition strategy feels reactive.
4. **Confidence Gap (7/10 severity)**: Needs to know "Creative C12 will fatigue in 3 days" with a confidence score, not "maybe it's declining."

**Goals**:
- Get 7-14 day advance visibility on creative fatigue
- Know exactly which budget moves to make tomorrow
- See confidence scores on everything (data nerd)
- Reduce CAC by 10% while maintaining LTV
- Get promoted to SVP Growth

**Job-to-be-Done**: "Creative C12 will fatigue in 3 days (confidence: 82%). TikTok cohort LTV dropped 8% (trend: declining). Shift $12K from TikTok â†’ Meta tomorrow. Give me the numbers, give me confidence, let me execute."

**Buying Behavior**:
- Decision Authority: **9/10** (Principal martech decision-maker, rarely overruled by CMO)
- Procurement Process: Recommends to CMO, CMO approves
- Decision Timeline: 1-3 weeks (fastest persona)
- Key Objection: "How accurate are the predictions? Show me the model performance."

## Subsector-Specific Context

### Beauty & Skincare (Rank #1 GTM Target)

**Why This Subsector First:**
- Highest pain concentration: acute post-iOS14 attribution loss
- Highest CAC inflation: 20% YoY average
- Fastest creative turnover: 2Ã— other verticals (7-14 day creative lifespan)
- Highest SKU churn: new product launches every month
- Extreme paid-social dependence: 70%+ of ad spend on Meta/TikTok

**Example Brands**:
- $65M-$285M subscription beauty boxes
- $80M-$150M DTC skincare brands (serums, cleansers, routines)
- $120M-$200M cosmetics (makeup, lip care)

**Unique Pain Points**:
- Visual creative fatigue is brutal (video ads burn out in 10-14 days)
- Influencer marketing attribution is a mess
- Seasonal product launches create budget chaos
- Customer acquisition cost vs LTV balance is critical (high LTV but high CAC)

> Source: 29. Unified 80-20 Pain Summary; 30. Validated ICP Summary; 33.GTM Wedge Strategy

---

### Supplements & Nutraceuticals (Rank #2)

**Why This Subsector:**
- Cross-platform attribution confusion (health claims complicate tracking)
- High LTV/CAC drift (subscription churn is volatile)
- Rampant media waste: 12%+ average wasted spend
- Compliance complexity (FDA, FTC guidelines)
- Growing TikTok volatility (algorithm changes hurt performance)

**Example Brands**:
- $70M-$180M vitamin/supplement subscriptions
- $90M-$150M protein/fitness supplements
- $110M-$200M wellness brands (probiotics, adaptogens)

**Unique Pain Points**:
- Health claims restrict creative freedom (compliance bottleneck)
- Subscription churn is unpredictable
- LTV calculation is complex (multiple purchase cycles)
- Platform policy changes impact ad delivery overnight

> Source: 29. Unified 80-20 Pain Summary; 30. Validated ICP Summary

---

### Health & Wellness (Rank #3)

**Why This Subsector:**
- High churn/LTV drift
- Omnichannel fragmentation (DTC + retail + Amazon)
- Growing TikTok/Instagram dependence
- Persistent margin challenges
- High LTV sensitivity due to subscription models

**Example Brands**:
- $85M-$220M fitness equipment/apparel
- $95M-$175M meditation/wellness apps
- $120M-$250M health food/beverage brands

**Unique Pain Points**:
- Omnichannel attribution is nearly impossible
- Retail partnerships complicate ROI calculations
- Customer lifetime varies wildly by segment
- Seasonal demand swings create budget volatility

> Source: 29. Unified 80-20 Pain Summary; 30. Validated ICP Summary
